What are Gamma Ray Bursts?

Gamma ray bursts, or GRBs, are surges of high frequency radiation from space detected by satellites. Resulting from the collapse of massive stars in distant galaxies, these events can last from fractions of a second to several hours and vary in intensity.

After being first observed in the 1960s, a lot has been learned about GRBs, their formation, and their effects. To date all GRBs have occurred outside of the Milky Way galaxy and have been harmless to earth.
